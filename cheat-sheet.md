## Exam Traps & Common Pitfalls

* **Prevalence vs. Severity:** DMFT/DMFS measures *severity/intensity* of caries experience, not prevalence. Total caries experience equals treated + untreated disease.
* **Diet vs. Nutrition:** Diet refers to *total intake* (what goes in the mouth), whereas nutrition refers to *absorption and systemic use*. Caries is primarily a diet issue (local substrate).
* **Arrested Lesion Appearance:** An arrested lesion does *not* have to be brown. The defining feature is a shiny/lustrous and intact/stable surface. Brown staining may occur but is not a diagnostic requirement.
* **SDF Patient Warnings:** Always warn patients about permanent black staining of the carious lesion before applying 38% SDF. Know the contraindications.
* **Pulpal Inflammation Sequence:** Bacterial *by-products* diffuse through dentinal tubules to cause pulpal inflammation *before* actual bacterial invasion of the pulp occurs.

## Professor Signals (Priority Concepts)

* **Critical pH:** Enamel demineralization is favored when pH drops below **5.5**.
* **Vipeholm Study:** Frequency and stickiness matter most. **Sticky sugar between meals** poses the highest cariogenic risk.
* **CRA Extreme Risk Definition:** A patient is only classified as "Extreme Risk" if they meet the criteria for High Risk (10+ points) **PLUS** confirmed severe dry mouth (salivary flow <0.7 ml/min).
* **Water Fluoridation Target:** The current target for systemic water fluoridation is **0.7 ppm**.
* **Pulpal Anatomy:** The pulp is a **low-compliance** environment with inefficient lymph drainage and a lack of collateral blood supply, making it highly susceptible to pressure necrosis from inflammation.

## Core Definitions & Fast-Discrimination Tables

### Pulpal Pain Fibers
| Fiber Type | Characteristics | Clinical Presentation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A-delta** | Myelinated, Low threshold | Sharp, pricking, cold (Reversible) |
| **C fibers** | Unmyelinated, High threshold | Burning, aching, lingering (Irreversible) |

| Risk Level | CRA Score | Recall Interval | Bitewing Interval |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low** | 0 points | 12 months | 24 months |
| **Moderate**| 1-9 points | 6 months | 18 months |
| **High** | 10+ points | 4 months | 12 months |
| **Extreme** | 10+ w/ dry mouth| 3 months | 6 months |

### Preventative Agent Escalation
| Risk Level | Daily Management Protocol |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Low** | Nutrition counseling + OTC Fluoride Toothpaste (2x/day) |
| **Moderate**| *Low Protocol* + OTC 0.05% NaF Rinse + 5% NaF Varnish |
| **High** | *Moderate Protocol* (Replace OTC paste with Rx 1.1% NaF / 5000 ppm) |
| **Extreme** | *High Protocol* + Dry-mouth rinse/buffers + Salivary/bacterial adjuncts |
